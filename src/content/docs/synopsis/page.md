## Step 4 — The One-Page Synopsis

*Take your paragraph synopsis and expand each sentence into its own paragraph. You now have five paragraphs — roughly one page. Disaster sentences each become two paragraphs: one for the lead-up, one for the disaster itself.*

:::tip[Goal]
By the end of this step you should have a clear sense of your three-act structure and the major turning points. If a section feels thin, that's the outline telling you there's a structural gap to fill.
:::

## Act One — Setup & Inciting Incident

[Expand the setup sentence. Who is your protagonist? What does their ordinary world look like? What do they want before the story forces their hand?]

[Expand the first disaster. What happens to shatter the status quo? What does the protagonist stand to lose?]

[Expand the second disaster. What goes wrong? How are the stakes raised?]

[Expand the third disaster / dark night lead-up. What is the protagonist's last attempt before everything collapses?]

[Expand the third disaster. What is lost? What does the protagonist believe is now impossible?]
