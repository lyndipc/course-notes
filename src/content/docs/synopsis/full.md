## Step 6 — The Four-Page Synopsis

*Expand your one-page synopsis by taking each paragraph and expanding it into roughly four paragraphs. This gives you a detailed, scene-by-scene roadmap of the entire novel — around four pages total.*

:::note[Before you start]
Complete Steps 3 and 5 (character sketches and synopses) before writing this. The characters you've built should now be driving the plot decisions, not the other way around.
:::

---

## Part One

[Detailed expansion of the opening act. Introduce your protagonist fully — their flaw, their wound, their want vs. need. Set the world. Establish tone.]

[The inciting incident in detail. What exactly happens? Who is involved? What does the protagonist decide to do about it, and why is that decision driven by their flaw?]

[The first act break. What commits the protagonist to the central conflict? What door closes behind them?]

---

## Part Two — First Half

[How does the protagonist pursue their goal? What allies and obstacles emerge? What does this cost them?]

[Midpoint event. Something shifts — either a false victory or a false defeat. The protagonist's approach must change.]

---

## Part Two — Second Half

[Stakes escalate. The protagonist doubles down, makes harder choices. Secondary characters exert pressure.]

[The all-is-lost moment / dark night of the soul. What is the lowest point? What truth does the protagonist have to face here?]

---

## Part Three

[The protagonist's internal shift. What do they now understand that they couldn't before? How does this change their plan?]

[The climax. The central conflict comes to a head. How does the protagonist use their changed self to act?]

[Resolution and denouement. What is the new normal? What has been won, lost, and transformed?]
