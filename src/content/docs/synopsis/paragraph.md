## Step 2 — The Five-Sentence Paragraph

*Expand your one-sentence premise into a single paragraph of approximately five sentences. Each sentence maps to a major structural beat.*

:::note[Structure]
1. **Setup** — Introduce the protagonist and their world.
2. **First disaster** — The inciting incident that ruptures the status quo.
3. **Second disaster** — The complication that raises the stakes and forces a harder choice.
4. **Third disaster / Dark night** — Everything seems lost; the protagonist hits bottom.
5. **Resolution** — How the story ends. You can be vague here — you just need to know you have one.
:::

## Notes on Structure

*Questions to pressure-test your paragraph:*

- Does each disaster escalate from the last?
- Is the protagonist actively making choices, or just reacting?
- Does the resolution feel earned by the journey?
- Is the tone consistent with your premise?
